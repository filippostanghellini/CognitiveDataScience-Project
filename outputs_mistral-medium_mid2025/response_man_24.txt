Choosing what to study at university is a big decision that depends on your **interests, strengths, career goals, financial considerations, and personal values**. ### **1. Future-Proof & High-Income Fields (Job Security + Salary)**
These fields are in demand now and projected to grow, often with strong salaries. Good if you prioritize **stability, earning potential, or STEM skills**.

#### **A. Technology & AI (Best for Analytical/Problem-Solvers)**
- **Computer Science** (Software Engineering, AI/ML, Cybersecurity, Data Science)
  - *Why?* Tech is eating the world. AI, cloud computing, and cybersecurity are booming.
  - *Careers:* Software engineer ($100K–$200K+), AI researcher, cybersecurity analyst.
  - *Alternatives:* **Information Systems, Computer Engineering, Robotics**.

- **Data Science / Statistics / Applied Math**
  - *Why?* Every industry needs data-driven decisions (finance, healthcare, marketing).
  - *Careers:* Data scientist ($120K–$180K), quant analyst, business intelligence.

#### **B. Engineering (For Builders & Problem-Solvers)**
- **Electrical/Computer Engineering** (Hardware, semiconductors, robotics)
- **Mechanical Engineering** (Automotive, aerospace, renewable energy)
- **Civil/Environmental Engineering** (Infrastructure, sustainability)
  - *Why?* Always in demand; engineering degrees are versatile.
  - *Careers:* $80K–$150K+ depending on specialization.

#### **C. Business & Finance (For Strategic Thinkers)**
- **Finance** (Investment banking, private equity, corporate finance)
- **Economics** (Policy, consulting, data economics)
- **Business Administration (MBA later?)** (Entrepreneurship, management)
  - *Why?* High earning potential in finance (e.g., investment banking: $150K+ starting).
  - *Careers:* Financial analyst, consultant, venture capitalist.

#### **D. Healthcare (Stable & Meaningful)**
- **Medicine (MD/DO)** – Long road (8+ years) but high prestige and income ($200K+).
- **Nursing** – High demand, faster entry ($70K–$120K).
- **Biomedical Engineering** – Mix of tech + healthcare.
- **Public Health** – Growing field post-pandemic.
  - *Why?* Aging population = job security.

#### **E. Skilled Trades (Alternative to University)**
- **Electrician, Plumbing, HVAC, Welding** – Often **higher early earnings** than many degrees, with less debt.
  - *Why?* Shortage of skilled workers; can start a business later.

### **2. Creative & Passion-Driven Fields (If Money Isn’t the Only Goal)**
If you’re more **artistic, expressive, or mission-driven**, consider:

#### **A. Design & Media**
- **Graphic Design / UX/UI Design** – Tech-adjacent creative field ($70K–$120K).
- **Film/Animation/Game Design** – Competitive but rewarding if talented.
- **Architecture** – Mix of art + engineering (requires licensing).

#### **B. Writing & Communication**
- **Journalism** (Risky but fulfilling; pivot to content marketing or PR).
- **Creative Writing** (Novels, screenwriting—hard to monetize but passion-based).
- **Marketing/Digital Media** – High demand for social media, SEO, and branding.

#### **C. Social Sciences & Humanities**
- **Psychology** (Therapy, HR, UX research—requires grad school for clinical work).
- **Philosophy** (Surprisingly good for law, tech ethics, or consulting).
- **History/Political Science** (Law, diplomacy, teaching).

#### **D. Entrepreneurship**
- Study **business, marketing, or CS**, then start a company.
- Alternatively, **drop out and build** (like many tech founders).

- **Quantum Computing** – Next-gen tech (physics/CS background).
- **Renewable Energy Engineering** – Solar, wind, battery tech.
- **Bioinformatics** – Mix of biology + data science (genomics, drug discovery).
- **Space Science/Aeronautics** – Private space industry (SpaceX, Blue Origin).
- **Ethics in AI/Tech** – Growing need for policy experts.

### **4. "Safe" Degrees (Versatile & Employable)**
If you’re **unsure**, these degrees keep doors open:
1. **Computer Science** (Can pivot to finance, healthcare, gaming, etc.)
2. **Business/Economics** (Flexible for corporate jobs or startups)
3. **Engineering** (Any type—teaches problem-solving)
4. **Statistics/Math** (Foundation for data science, finance, academia)
5. **Communications** (Marketing, PR, HR, media)

---
### **5. What to Avoid (Unless You’re Passionate)**
- **Fine Arts** (Unless you have a clear plan—e.g., commercial illustration).
- **Theater/Acting** (Extremely competitive; side hustles required).
- **Generic "Studies" Degrees** (e.g., "Communications Studies" without a skill focus).
- **Degrees with Saturated Job Markets** (e.g., law unless you go to a top school).

---
### **How to Decide? Ask Yourself:**
1. **What am I naturally good at?** (Math? Writing? Building?)
2. **What problems do I enjoy solving?** (Technical? Social? Creative?)
3. **Do I want stability or am I okay with risk?**
4. **Do I need high income, or is fulfillment more important?**
5. **Am I willing to go to grad school?** (Medicine, law, academia require it.)

### **Pro Tips:**
- **Double major or minor** in something practical (e.g., CS + Economics, Bio + Data Science).
- **Learn coding** (Python, SQL) no matter your field—it’s a superpower.
- **Internships > GPA** – Real-world experience matters more.
- **Consider community college first** to save money, then transfer.
- **Gap year?** Try coding bootcamps (e.g., LeetCode for CS), travel, or work to clarify your path.
