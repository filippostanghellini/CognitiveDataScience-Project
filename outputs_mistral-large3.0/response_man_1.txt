The best field of study for you depends on your interests, strengths, career goals, and the job market. Here are some broad recommendations based on different priorities:

### **1. High-Demand & Future-Proof Fields**
If you want strong job prospects and stability, consider:
- **Computer Science / Software Engineering** – High salaries, remote work options, and growing demand (AI, cybersecurity, cloud computing, etc.).
- **Data Science / Analytics** – Combines stats, programming, and business insights (great for finance, tech, healthcare).
- **Engineering (Electrical, Mechanical, Civil, Chemical)** – Always in demand, especially in renewable energy, robotics, and infrastructure.
- **Healthcare (Medicine, Nursing, Biomedical Sciences, Public Health)** – Recession-proof, especially with aging populations.
- **Artificial Intelligence / Machine Learning** – Cutting-edge field with applications in nearly every industry.

### **2. High-Earning Potential**
If salary is a priority:
- **Finance (Investment Banking, Quantitative Finance, Actuarial Science)** – High-paying but competitive.
- **Petroleum / Mining Engineering** – Lucrative but volatile (depends on oil/gas markets).
- **Management Consulting (Business, Economics, MBA later)** – High salaries with top firms (McKinsey, BCG, Bain).
- **Law (Corporate, Intellectual Property, Tech Law)** – Requires a JD (law degree) but can be very rewarding.
- **Dentistry / Medicine** – Long training but high earning potential (especially in specialties).

### **3. Passion & Creativity**
If you want a fulfilling career aligned with your interests:
- **Psychology / Neuroscience** – If you love understanding human behavior.
- **Architecture / Urban Planning** – For creative, design-oriented minds.
- **Environmental Science / Sustainability** – If you care about climate change and green tech.
- **Philosophy / Political Science** – Great for critical thinking, law, or academia.
- **Game Design / Digital Arts** – If you love gaming, animation, or VR.
- **Music / Performing Arts** – Competitive but rewarding if you're talented and persistent.

### **4. Entrepreneurship & Business**
If you want to start your own business or work in leadership:
- **Business Administration / Entrepreneurship** – General business skills.
- **Marketing / Digital Marketing** – Great for creative, data-driven minds.
- **Supply Chain / Logistics** – Growing field with global opportunities.
- **Economics** – Strong analytical skills for finance, policy, or consulting.

### **5. Trades & Alternative Paths**
If university isn’t the right fit, consider:
- **Apprenticeships (Electrician, Plumbing, HVAC, Welding)** – High demand, good pay, no student debt.
- **Coding Bootcamps** – Fast track to tech jobs (3-6 months).
- **Military / Police / Firefighting** – Structured career paths with benefits.

### **Questions to Help You Decide:**
✅ **What subjects do you enjoy most?** (Math, science, writing, art, etc.)
✅ **What kind of work environment do you prefer?** (Office, outdoors, lab, remote, etc.)
✅ **Do you want a high salary, job security, or work-life balance?**
✅ **Are you open to grad school (MBA, JD, MD, PhD) or do you want to enter the workforce fast?**

### **Final Advice:**
- **If unsure, start with a general degree (Business, Economics, Psychology, Computer Science) and specialize later.**
- **Look at job growth projections** (e.g., U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, LinkedIn trends).
- **Talk to professionals** in fields you’re curious about (LinkedIn is great for this).
- **Consider internships early** to test different careers.
