### **1. STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, Math) – High Demand & Impact**
Women are still underrepresented in many STEM fields, but these areas offer **high salaries, job security, and the chance to innovate**. If you enjoy problem-solving, analytics, or creativity in tech/science, consider:
- **Computer Science / Software Engineering**
  - *Why?* Tech is booming, remote work is common, and you can specialize in AI, cybersecurity, UX design, or data science.
  - *Careers:* Software developer, AI engineer, product manager, tech entrepreneur.
  - *Women in Tech:* Organizations like **Girls Who Code** and **Women Who Tech** offer support.

- **Data Science / Statistics**
  - *Why?* Data drives decisions in every industry (healthcare, finance, marketing). Strong growth and flexibility.
  - *Careers:* Data scientist, business analyst, research scientist.

- **Engineering (Biomedical, Environmental, Chemical, Electrical)**
  - *Why?* Hands-on problem-solving with real-world impact (e.g., medical devices, sustainable energy).
  - *Women in Engineering:* **Society of Women Engineers (SWE)** provides networking.

- **Biotechnology / Bioinformatics**
  - *Why?* Merge biology with tech to work on vaccines, gene editing (CRISPR), or personalized medicine.

- **Mathematics / Actuarial Science**
  - *Why?* High-paying roles in finance, insurance, or academia with strong work-life balance.

### **2. Healthcare & Medicine – Meaningful & Stable**
If you’re passionate about helping others and enjoy science, healthcare offers **job security and purpose**:
- **Medicine (MD/DO)**
  - *Specialties:* Pediatrics, obstetrics/gynecology, psychiatry, surgery.
  - *Note:* Long training (4 years med school + residency), but deeply rewarding.

- **Nursing (BSN, Nurse Practitioner, Midwifery)**
  - *Why?* High demand, flexible schedules (shift work), and leadership opportunities.
  - *Growth Areas:* Nurse informatics (tech + healthcare), travel nursing.

- **Public Health / Epidemiology**
  - *Why?* Work on global health, pandemics, or health policy (e.g., WHO, CDC).
  - *Careers:* Health educator, epidemiologist, NGO program director.

- **Pharmacy (PharmD) or Pharmaceutical Sciences**
  - *Why?* Stable field with roles in hospitals, research, or drug development.

- **Physical Therapy / Occupational Therapy**
  - *Why?* Hands-on, patient-centered, and growing due to aging populations.

### **3. Business & Finance – Leadership & Flexibility**
If you’re strategic, analytical, or entrepreneurial, business fields offer **high earning potential and diversity**:
- **Business Administration (MBA later)**
  - *Specializations:* Marketing, operations, human resources, or entrepreneurship.
  - *Careers:* Consultant, product manager, CEO, startup founder.

- **Finance / Economics**
  - *Why?* Lucrative roles in investing, corporate finance, or fintech.
  - *Careers:* Financial analyst, investment banker, risk manager.
  - *Women in Finance:* **Ellevate Network**, **85 Broads**.

- **Accounting**
  - *Why?* Always in demand, clear career path (CPA), and remote work options.

- **Supply Chain Management**
  - *Why?* Critical for global trade; high demand post-pandemic.

### **4. Social Sciences & Humanities – Advocacy & Creativity**
If you’re passionate about people, culture, or social change:
- **Psychology**
  - *Careers:* Clinical psychologist (PhD/PsyD), industrial-organizational psychologist (corporate roles), counseling.
  - *Growth Area:* Mental health tech (e.g., therapy apps).

- **Sociology / Anthropology**
  - *Why?* Study human behavior; apply to HR, market research, or NGOs.

- **International Relations / Political Science**
  - *Careers:* Diplomat, policy analyst, NGO worker, lawyer (with further study).

- **Law (JD)**
  - *Specializations:* Human rights, corporate law, environmental law.
  - *Note:* Competitive but versatile.

- **Communications / Media Studies**
  - *Careers:* Journalism, PR, digital marketing, content creation.
  - *Growth Area:* Social media strategy, podcasting, influencer marketing.

### **Factors to Consider When Choosing:**
1. **Passion vs. Practicality:** Do you want to follow a calling (e.g., art, nonprofit work) or prioritize job stability (e.g., healthcare, tech)?
2. **Work-Life Balance:**
   - *Flexible:* Tech (remote), nursing (shift choices), freelance (design, writing).
   - *Demanding:* Law, medicine, investment banking.
3. **Salary Potential:**
   - High: Tech, finance, medicine, engineering.
   - Moderate: Education, social work, arts.
4. **Job Growth:**
   - Fastest-growing: Data science, healthcare, renewable energy, AI.
5. **Gender Representation:**
   - Women-dominated: Nursing, education, HR.
   - Male-dominated (but changing): Engineering, computer science, trades.
6. **Further Education Needed?**
   - Undergrad-only: Tech, business, communications.
   - Grad school required: Medicine, law, psychology (for clinical roles).
