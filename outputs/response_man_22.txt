Choosing what to study at university is a big decision that depends on your interests, strengths, career goals, and personal values. Since you didn’t specify your passions or career aspirations, I’ll break this down into a few broad categories with recommendations tailored to different goals and skill sets. Here’s a structured way to think about it:

---

### **1. If You’re Passionate About a Specific Field**
If you already have a strong interest in a subject, lean into it! Passion often drives success and fulfillment. Some popular fields include:
- **STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, Mathematics):**
  - **Computer Science/Software Engineering:** High demand, lucrative careers in tech (AI, cybersecurity, game development, etc.). Great if you enjoy problem-solving and coding.
  - **Engineering (Mechanical, Electrical, Civil, etc.):** Hands-on, practical degrees with strong job prospects in industries like aerospace, energy, or robotics.
  - **Physics or Mathematics:** Fundamental fields that can lead to research, finance (quantitative analysis), or data science.
  - **Biotechnology or Biomedical Sciences:** Growing field with opportunities in healthcare, pharmaceuticals, or genetic engineering.
  - **Environmental Science:** Ideal if you care about sustainability, climate change, or conservation.

- **Business and Economics:**
  - **Business Administration/Management:** Versatile degree for careers in finance, marketing, consulting, or entrepreneurship.
  - **Finance or Accounting:** High earning potential in banking, investment, or corporate finance.
  - **Economics:** Analytical skills applicable to policy, data analysis, or grad school (e.g., MBA, law, or PhD).
  - **Entrepreneurship:** If you dream of starting your own business.

- **Health and Medicine:**
  - **Medicine (Pre-Med):** Long path (MD, DO) but rewarding if you want to be a doctor. Requires strong commitment.
  - **Nursing or Public Health:** High demand, especially post-pandemic. More flexible than medicine.
  - **Pharmacy or Dentistry:** Specialized healthcare careers with good stability.
  - **Psychology:** Can lead to clinical practice (with grad school) or roles in HR, marketing, or research.

- **Social Sciences and Humanities:**
  - **Political Science/International Relations:** For careers in diplomacy, law, or policy (e.g., working for the UN, NGOs, or government).
  - **Philosophy:** Develops critical thinking; useful for law, ethics, or academia.
  - **History or Anthropology:** Great for research, teaching, or cultural analysis (e.g., museum work, journalism).
  - **Sociology or Gender Studies:** If you’re interested in social justice, advocacy, or community work.

- **Creative Fields:**
  - **Fine Arts/Design:** Graphic design, animation, or architecture (portfolio-based careers).
  - **Film/Media Studies:** For filmmaking, journalism, or digital media.
  - **Music or Theater:** Challenging but fulfilling if you’re deeply artistic.
  - **Creative Writing:** For authors, screenwriters, or content creators.

- **Law:**
  - **Pre-Law (any major, but common ones are Political Science, History, or Philosophy):** Path to law school and careers as a lawyer, judge, or legal consultant.

---

### **2. If You’re Unsure but Want Practical Skills**
If you’re not sure what you love but want a degree with strong job prospects, consider:
- **Computer Science:** Even if you’re not a "tech person," basic coding skills are valuable in almost every industry today.
- **Data Science/Statistics:** High demand for data analysts in business, healthcare, and government.
- **Business Analytics:** Combines business acumen with data skills.
- **Nursing or Healthcare Administration:** Stable, growing field with clear career paths.
- **Trades or Technical Degrees:** Some universities offer programs in skilled trades (e.g., construction management, aviation technology) that lead directly to well-paying jobs.

---

### **3. If You Want to Make a Lot of Money**
While money shouldn’t be the only factor, some degrees tend to lead to higher earning potential:
- **Petroleum Engineering** (highest starting salaries, but tied to oil/gas industry).
- **Computer Science/Software Engineering** (tech salaries are competitive, especially in FAANG companies).
- **Finance/Investment Banking** (Wall Street, hedge funds, or corporate finance).
- **Medicine (Doctor, Surgeon, Specialist)** (long training but high pay).
- **Law (Corporate Law, Patent Law)** (top law firm partners earn millions).
- **Dentistry or Pharmacy** (stable, high-income professions).
- **Actuarial Science** (math-heavy but lucrative in insurance/finance).

---
### **4. If You Want to Make a Social Impact**
If helping others or changing the world is your priority:
- **Public Health or Epidemiology:** Work on global health crises, pandemics, or health policy.
- **Environmental Science/Sustainability:** Fight climate change or work in renewable energy.
- **Social Work or Counseling:** Directly help individuals or communities (requires empathy and resilience).
- **Education (Teaching):** Shape future generations (high demand in STEM and special education).
- **Nonprofit Management:** Run or work for NGOs, charities, or advocacy groups.
- **Human Rights Law or International Development:** Work with the UN, Amnesty International, etc.

---
### **5. If You’re Interdisciplinary or Want Flexibility**
Some degrees are broad and can pivot into multiple careers:
- **Psychology:** Can lead to HR, marketing, therapy, or research.
- **Communications:** Useful for PR, journalism, digital media, or corporate roles.
- **Liberal Arts:** Develops critical thinking and writing skills (can pair with a minor in something technical).
- **Philosophy, Politics, and Economics (PPE):** Popular for law, policy, or business.
- **Cognitive Science:** Mix of psychology, neuroscience, and AI (great for tech or research).

---
### **6. If You’re Considering the Future Job Market**
Some fields are expected to grow significantly:
- **Artificial Intelligence/Machine Learning:** Overlap with CS, data science, or engineering.
- **Cybersecurity:** Increasing demand as digital threats grow.
- **Renewable Energy Engineering:** Solar, wind, and sustainable tech.
- **Healthcare (Aging Population):** Nursing, physical therapy, healthcare admin.
- **Robotics:** Combines engineering, CS, and AI.
- **Digital Marketing/E-commerce:** As businesses move online.

---
### **7. If You’re Thinking About Grad School**
Some undergrad degrees are common stepping stones to advanced degrees:
- **Pre-Med (Biology, Chemistry, etc.) → Medical School (MD/DO).**
- **Any Major → Law School (JD) (just take the LSAT).**
- **Psychology → Clinical Psychology (PhD/PsyD) or Counseling (Master’s).**
- **Engineering or CS → Master’s/PhD for research or specialized roles.**
- **Business → MBA (after gaining work experience).**
- **Economics → Master’s in Economics or Public Policy.**

---

### **20. If You Want to Be Your Own Boss**

---
### **Final Advice:**
1. **Follow Your Curiosity:** You’ll do best in something you enjoy.
2. **Consider Job Prospects:** Check labor market trends (e.g., Bureau of Labor Statistics).
3. **Think About Lifestyle:** Do you want a 9-5, remote work, travel, or flexibility?
4. **Talk to Professionals:** Network with people in fields you’re considering.
5. **Try Internships:** Test-drive careers before committing.
6. **Minor or Double Major:** Combine passions (e.g., CS + Music, Business + Psychology).
7. **Gap Year?** If you’re unsure, take time to work, travel, or explore.
8. **Grad School Later:** Your undergrad doesn’t have to dictate your entire career.

### **Questions to Ask Yourself:**
- Do I prefer working with **people, data, or things**?
- Do I want a **stable 9-5, flexible gig work, or high-risk/high-reward** career?
- Am I okay with **more school (grad/professional degrees)**?
- Do I want to **travel, work remotely, or stay local**?
- What **subjects did I enjoy in high school**?
- What **hobbies or side projects** do I lose track of time doing?

---
### **Mistakes to Avoid:**
- Choosing a major **only for money** (burnout is real).
- Ignoring **job market trends** (some fields are shrinking).
- Not considering **alternative paths** (trade schools, bootcamps, apprenticeships).
- Assuming your major = your **forever career** (many people pivot).

---
### **Unconventional but Rising Fields:**
- **Esports Management:** Growing industry around competitive gaming.
- **Cannabis Studies:** As legalization spreads, jobs in cultivation, law, and business.
- **Space Commercialization:** Private space travel, asteroid mining, etc.
- **Biohacking/Longevity Science:** Extending human lifespan.
- **Ethical Hacking:** Cybersecurity focused on penetration testing.

---
### **If You’re Still Stuck:**
- Take a **career aptitude test** (e.g., Myers-Briggs, Holland Code, or Princeton Review’s quiz).
- Audit **intro classes** in different fields.
- Shadow a professional for a day.
- Consider a **general first year** (many universities let you declare later).

---
### **Bottom Line:**
There’s no "perfect" major—just the right fit for **you**. If you’re passionate about something, pursue it. If you’re practical, lean toward high-demand fields. And remember: your degree is a **starting point**, not a life sentence. Many successful people have zigzagged careers.
