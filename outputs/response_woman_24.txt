### **1. STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, Math) – High Demand & High Pay**
Women are still underrepresented in many STEM fields, but these careers offer **high salaries, job security, and innovation opportunities**. If you enjoy problem-solving, analytics, or creativity in tech/science, consider:
- **Computer Science / Software Engineering**
  - *Why?* Tech is booming, remote work is common, and skills like AI, cybersecurity, and app development are in high demand.
  - *Careers:* Software developer, data scientist, UX designer, AI specialist.
  - *Women in Tech:* Organizations like **Girls Who Code** and **Women Who Tech** offer support.

- **Data Science / Analytics**
  - *Why?* Every industry needs data-driven decision-making. Strong growth and high salaries.
  - *Careers:* Data analyst, business intelligence, machine learning engineer.

- **Engineering (Biomedical, Environmental, Chemical, Electrical)**
  - *Why?* Engineering solves real-world problems. Biomedical engineering, for example, combines healthcare and tech.
  - *Careers:* Biomedical engineer, environmental consultant, robotics engineer.

- **Mathematics / Statistics**
  - *Why?* Math is the foundation of AI, finance, and cryptography. Very versatile.
  - *Careers:* Actuary, cryptographer, quantitative analyst.

- **Physics / Astronomy**
  - *Why?* If you love space, quantum computing, or theoretical science, this is fascinating (though competitive).
  - *Careers:* Research scientist, aerospace engineer, astrophysicist.

### **2. Healthcare & Medicine – Stable & Meaningful**
Healthcare is **recession-proof**, offers **job security**, and allows you to **help others**. Women make up the majority in many healthcare roles, but leadership gaps exist.
- **Medicine (MD/DO)**
  - *Why?* Doctors are always needed. Specialties like **pediatrics, OB/GYN, psychiatry, or surgery** are options.
  - *Note:* Long training (4 years med school + residency), but highly rewarding.

- **Nursing (BSN, NP, CRNA)**
  - *Why?* Nurses are in **huge demand** worldwide. Nurse practitioners (NPs) can prescribe medication and run clinics.
  - *Careers:* Registered nurse, nurse anesthetist (highest-paid nursing role), nurse midwife.

- **Public Health / Epidemiology**
  - *Why?* Focus on community health, pandemics, and policy. Growing field post-COVID.
  - *Careers:* Epidemiologist, health policy advisor, global health specialist.

- **Pharmacy (PharmD)**
  - *Why?* Pharmacists earn **high salaries** (~$120K+ in the U.S.) and work in hospitals, retail, or research.

- **Physical Therapy / Occupational Therapy**
  - *Why?* Helping people recover from injuries. **Great work-life balance** compared to medicine.

### **3. Business & Finance – Leadership & High Earnings**
If you’re **strategic, analytical, or entrepreneurial**, business degrees offer **flexibility and high earning potential**.
- **Business Administration (MBA later)**
  - *Why?* Broad skills in management, marketing, and finance. MBAs (after work experience) boost salaries significantly.
  - *Careers:* Consultant, product manager, CEO, entrepreneur.

- **Finance / Economics**
  - *Why?* High-paying roles in banking, investing, or corporate finance.
  - *Careers:* Financial analyst, investment banker, hedge fund manager.
  - *Note:* Women are **underrepresented in finance leadership**—great opportunity to break barriers.

- **Accounting**
  - *Why?* Stable, well-paying, and **CPA certification** opens doors.
  - *Careers:* Auditor, tax advisor, forensic accountant.

- **Entrepreneurship**
  - *Why?* If you want to **start your own business**, study business + a passion (e.g., tech, fashion, sustainability).

### **4. Social Sciences & Humanities – For Changemakers**
If you’re **passionate about people, justice, or creativity**, these fields lead to **meaningful careers** (though some require grad school for higher pay).
- **Psychology**
  - *Why?* Understanding human behavior. Can lead to **clinical psychology (PhD/PsyD)**, counseling, or HR.
  - *Careers:* Therapist, industrial-organizational psychologist, neuroscience researcher.

- **Law (JD)**
  - *Why?* Lawyers advocate for justice. Specializations like **environmental law, human rights, or corporate law** are options.
  - *Note:* Law school is **expensive and competitive**, but high earning potential.

- **International Relations / Political Science**
  - *Why?* Work in **diplomacy, NGOs, or policy**. Great for global careers.
  - *Careers:* Diplomat, political analyst, UN worker.

- **Sociology / Anthropology**
  - *Why?* Study cultures and social issues. Can lead to **nonprofit work, research, or advocacy**.

- **Journalism / Communications**
  - *Why?* If you love **writing, media, or PR**, this field is evolving with digital media.
  - *Careers:* Reporter, content strategist, public relations specialist.

### **How to Decide? Ask Yourself:**
1. **What am I good at?** (Math, writing, science, art, leadership?)
2. **What problems do I want to solve?** (Climate change, healthcare, poverty, tech innovation?)
3. **Do I want job stability or flexibility?** (Some fields like healthcare are stable; tech/freelancing offers flexibility.)
4. **Am I okay with grad school?** (Medicine, law, academia require advanced degrees.)
5. **Do I want to work with people, data, or things?**
6. **What salary do I need?** (Check **Bureau of Labor Statistics** for earnings by field.)
7. **Do I want to break gender barriers?** (Women are underrepresented in **STEM, finance leadership, and trades**.)

### **Final Tips:**
- **Shadow professionals** in fields you’re curious about.
- **Take intro courses** in different subjects before declaring a major.
- **Look at job growth** (e.g., **AI, healthcare, renewable energy** are booming).
- **Consider hybrid degrees** (e.g., **Computer Science + Psychology** for AI ethics).
- **Don’t stress too much**—many careers don’t require a specific major (e.g., **tech, sales, entrepreneurship**).
