### **1. STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, Math) – High Demand & Impact**
If you enjoy problem-solving, innovation, or working with data/technology, STEM fields offer **high salaries, job security, and global opportunities**. Women are still a minority in many STEM areas, so your contribution could be especially valuable.
- **Computer Science / AI / Data Science** – Tech is booming, and skills in coding, machine learning, or cybersecurity are in huge demand. Women like **Reshma Saujani (Girls Who Code)** and **Fei-Fei Li (AI pioneer)** are leading the way.
- **Engineering (Biomedical, Environmental, Chemical, Electrical)** – Great for hands-on problem solvers. Women engineers often work on **sustainable tech, healthcare innovations, or renewable energy**.
- **Mathematics / Statistics** – Foundational for finance, cryptography, and research. **Katherine Johnson (NASA mathematician)** is a legendary example.
- **Physics / Astronomy** – If you love space or theoretical science, this could lead to research or aerospace careers.

**Why?** STEM fields are **future-proof**, often allow remote work, and many companies are actively **seeking to hire more women**.

### **2. Health & Medicine – Helping Others & Job Stability**
If you’re passionate about **healthcare, biology, or mental well-being**, these fields are **recession-resistant** and deeply rewarding.
- **Medicine (Doctor, Surgeon, Psychiatrist)** – Long but fulfilling path. Women now make up **~50% of medical students** in many countries.
- **Nursing / Midwifery** – High demand worldwide, with opportunities to specialize (e.g., **nurse practitioner, neonatal care**).
- **Public Health / Epidemiology** – Focus on **global health, pandemics, or health policy** (think **Dr. Anthony Fauci’s field**).
- **Biotechnology / Genetics** – Work on **gene editing (CRISPR), vaccines, or personalized medicine**.
- **Psychology / Neuroscience** – Study the **brain, behavior, or mental health** (great for therapy, research, or HR roles).

**Why?** Healthcare is **always needed**, and women often excel in **empathetic, patient-centered roles**.

**Why?** Business skills are **versatile**—you can pivot into **any industry**, and companies are pushing for **more women in C-suite roles**.

**Why?** These careers offer **job security, good pay, and often flexibility**.
