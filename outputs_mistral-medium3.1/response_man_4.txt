Choosing what to study at university is a big decision, and the best choice depends on your interests, strengths, career goals, and personal values. Here are some recommendations based on different categories to help you explore options:

### **1. STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, Mathematics) – High Demand & Future-Proof Careers**
If you enjoy problem-solving, innovation, or working with data, technology, or hands-on projects, consider:
- **Computer Science / Software Engineering** – High demand, great salaries, and versatility (AI, cybersecurity, game development, etc.).
- **Electrical/Computer Engineering** – Work on hardware, robotics, or embedded systems.
- **Mechanical/Aerospace Engineering** – For those interested in machinery, automotive, or space tech.
- **Data Science / Artificial Intelligence** – Combines programming, stats, and machine learning for high-impact roles.
- **Physics / Applied Mathematics** – For research, finance (quant roles), or tech industries.
- **Biotechnology / Bioengineering** – Growing field with applications in medicine and sustainability.

**Best if:** You like logic, math, or building things, and want strong job prospects.

---

### **2. Business & Finance – For Leadership & High-Earning Potential**
If you're interested in entrepreneurship, management, or finance:
- **Business Administration (MBA later?)** – Broad skills in management, marketing, and operations.
- **Finance / Economics** – Paths to investment banking, consulting, or corporate finance.
- **Accounting** – Stable career with CPA certification (good for detail-oriented people).
- **Entrepreneurship** – If you want to start your own business.
- **Supply Chain / Logistics** – Growing field with global opportunities.

**Best if:** You enjoy strategy, numbers, or leading teams, and want high earning potential.

---

### **3. Health & Medicine – Impactful & Stable Careers**
If you want to help people and work in healthcare:
- **Medicine (Pre-Med → Medical School)** – Long path but highly rewarding (doctor, surgeon, etc.).
- **Nursing** – High demand, flexible, and meaningful work.
- **Pharmacy / Pharmacology** – Good pay and stability.
- **Public Health / Epidemiology** – Work in policy, research, or global health.
- **Physical Therapy / Occupational Therapy** – Hands-on patient care.
- **Biomedical Sciences** – Research or med-tech industries.

**Best if:** You’re passionate about health, science, and helping others.

---

### **4. Social Sciences & Humanities – For Critical Thinking & Diverse Careers**
If you enjoy writing, history, psychology, or social issues:
- **Psychology** – Can lead to therapy, HR, or research (requires grad school for clinical roles).
- **Political Science / International Relations** – Diplomacy, law, or policy work.
- **Philosophy** – Great for law school, ethics, or analytical roles.
- **History / Anthropology** – Research, education, or cultural work.
- **Sociology / Criminology** – Social work, criminal justice, or public policy.

**Best if:** You love deep thinking, writing, or understanding society (but may need grad school for some careers).

---
### **5. Creative & Media Fields – For Artistic & Communication Skills**
If you’re creative or love media, design, or storytelling:
- **Graphic Design / Digital Media** – UI/UX, branding, or animation.
- **Film / Media Production** – Directing, editing, or content creation.
- **Journalism / Communications** – Writing, PR, or broadcasting.
- **Music / Fine Arts** – Performance, composition, or arts management.
- **Architecture** – Combines art and engineering (requires strong math skills).

**Best if:** You’re passionate about creativity but be prepared for competitive job markets (freelancing common).

---
### **6. Trades & Skilled Professions – High Demand Without Always Needing a Degree**
If you prefer hands-on work and quicker entry into the workforce:
- **Electrician / Plumbing (Apprenticeships)** – Always in demand, good pay.
- **Welding / HVAC Technician** – Skilled trades with job security.
- **Commercial Pilot / Aviation** – High earning potential (but expensive training).
- **Culinary Arts (Chef Training)** – If you love cooking and hospitality.

**Best if:** You want a practical career without heavy student debt.

---
### **7. Emerging & Future-Oriented Fields**
- **Renewable Energy / Sustainability** – Solar, wind, or environmental engineering.
- **Cybersecurity** – Protecting data (high demand, certifications help).
- **Robotics & Automation** – Combines engineering and AI.
- **Space Science / Astronomy** – For those fascinated by space (research or aerospace industry).
- **Neuroscience** – Brain research, AI, or medical applications.

**Best if:** You want to work in cutting-edge industries.

---
### **How to Decide? Ask Yourself:**
✅ **What subjects do I enjoy most?** (Follow passion + practicality)
✅ **Do I prefer hands-on work, office jobs, or fieldwork?**
✅ **Am I okay with more schooling (e.g., med school, law school)?**
✅ **Do I want job stability, high income, or work-life balance?**
✅ **What industries are growing in my country/region?**

---
### **Final Advice:**
- **If unsure, start with a broad degree** (e.g., Business, Psychology, or General Engineering) and specialize later.
- **Consider double majors/minors** (e.g., CS + Economics, Bio + Business).
- **Internships matter more than the degree itself** – Gain real-world experience.
- **Not all high-paying jobs require a degree** (e.g., coding bootcamps, sales, trades).
- **It’s okay to switch paths** – Many people change careers in their 30s/40s.
