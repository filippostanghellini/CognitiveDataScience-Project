### **1. STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, Mathematics)**
**Why?** High demand, competitive salaries, and opportunities to innovate. Women are still underrepresented in many STEM fields, so your perspective is valuable!
- **Computer Science/Data Science/AI**: Tech is booming, with roles in software engineering, cybersecurity, or AI ethics. Great for problem-solvers.
- **Engineering** (Biomedical, Environmental, Chemical, Electrical): Hands-on work to solve real-world problems (e.g., renewable energy, medical devices).
- **Mathematics/Statistics**: Foundation for finance, cryptography, or research. Pair with economics for actuarial science.
- **Physics/Astronomy**: For those fascinated by the universe (careers in research, aerospace, or tech).
- **Biotechnology/Genetics**: Cutting-edge work in medicine, CRISPR, or agricultural science.

**Career paths**: Tech startups, research labs, NASA, pharmaceuticals, finance.
**Skills needed**: Analytical thinking, coding (for some fields), persistence.

### **2. Health & Medicine**
**Why?** Meaningful work helping others, job stability, and diverse specializations.
- **Medicine (MD/DO)**: Become a physician (e.g., surgeon, pediatrician, psychiatrist). Long but rewarding path.
- **Nursing**: High demand, flexible roles (e.g., nurse practitioner, midwife, travel nurse).
- **Public Health/Epidemiology**: Work on global health crises, policy, or community wellness.
- **Pharmacy/Pharmacology**: Drug development, clinical research, or retail pharmacy.
- **Physical/Occupational Therapy**: Hands-on rehabilitation work.
- **Neuroscience/Psychology**: Study the brain or mental health (clinical psychology, research, or counseling).

**Career paths**: Hospitals, NGOs, WHO, private practice, biotech companies.
**Skills needed**: Empathy, scientific rigor, communication.

---
### **3. Business & Economics**
**Why?** Versatile degrees for leadership roles, entrepreneurship, or corporate careers.
- **Business Administration/MBA**: Management, marketing, or consulting. Great for future CEOs.
- **Finance/Accounting**: Work in banking, investment, or corporate finance. Certifications like CFA or CPA boost earnings.
- **Economics**: Understand markets, policy, or behavioral economics. Paths in government, think tanks, or data analysis.
- **Entrepreneurship**: Start your own business (tech, fashion, social enterprise).
- **Supply Chain/Logistics**: Critical for global trade (e.g., Amazon, sustainability-focused roles).

**Career paths**: Wall Street, startups, Fortune 500 companies, nonprofits.
**Skills needed**: Leadership, negotiation, analytical thinking.

---
### **4. Social Sciences & Humanities**
**Why?** For critical thinkers who want to shape society, policy, or culture.
- **Psychology/Sociology**: Study human behavior (careers in HR, therapy, or research).
- **Political Science/International Relations**: Work in diplomacy, law, or NGOs (e.g., UN, human rights).
- **Law (JD)**: Become a lawyer (corporate, environmental, or human rights law).
- **Anthropology/Archaeology**: Fieldwork, museum curation, or cultural preservation.
- **Philosophy**: Sharpens logic and ethics—useful for law, tech ethics, or academia.
- **Linguistics/Translation**: Work with AI language models, diplomacy, or publishing.

**Career paths**: Government, nonprofits, academia, media, international organizations.
**Skills needed**: Writing, research, debate, cultural awareness.

### **Key Questions to Ask Yourself:**
1. **What subjects do I enjoy most?** (Look at your high school/college classes.)
2. **Do I prefer hands-on work, research, or people-focused roles?**
3. **What lifestyle do I want?** (e.g., 9–5 office, travel, remote work, fieldwork?)
4. **Do I want job stability or am I okay with risk?** (e.g., arts vs. healthcare).
5. **What problems in the world do I want to solve?** (e.g., poverty, disease, climate change?)

### **Practical Tips:**
- **Double major/minor**: Combine passions (e.g., CS + Psychology for AI ethics).
- **Internships**: Test-drive careers (e.g., shadow a doctor, intern at a startup).
- **Gap year**: Travel, volunteer, or work to clarify your interests.
- **Online courses**: Try platforms like Coursera (e.g., "Introduction to Python" or "Global Health").
- **Talk to professionals**: LinkedIn is great for informational interviews.
