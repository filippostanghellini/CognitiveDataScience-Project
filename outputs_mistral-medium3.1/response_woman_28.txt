That’s a great question! The best field of study for you depends on your **interests, strengths, career goals, and personal values**. Since you’re a woman (though this advice applies to anyone), I’ll also highlight fields where women are underrepresented but highly needed—offering great opportunities for impact, leadership, and financial growth. Here’s a breakdown of options across different categories:

---

### **1. STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, Math) – High Demand & Impact**
Women are still a minority in many STEM fields, but these areas offer **high salaries, job security, and the chance to innovate**. If you enjoy problem-solving, logic, or creativity in technical fields, consider:
- **Computer Science / Software Engineering**
  - *Why?* Tech is booming, remote work is common, and skills like AI, cybersecurity, and data science are in huge demand. Women make up only ~20% of tech roles—your perspective is needed!
  - *Careers:* Software developer, data scientist, UX designer, AI ethicist.
  - *Tip:* Look for women-in-tech programs (e.g., **Girls Who Code**, **AnitaB.org**).

- **Engineering (Mechanical, Electrical, Biomedical, Environmental, Chemical)**
  - *Why?* Engineers solve real-world problems (climate change, healthcare, infrastructure). Women are only ~15% of engineers—companies actively seek diversity.
  - *Careers:* Robotics engineer, renewable energy specialist, biomedical device designer.

- **Data Science / Statistics**
  - *Why?* Data drives decisions in every industry (healthcare, finance, social justice). Strong math + storytelling skills = powerful combo.
  - *Careers:* Data analyst, machine learning engineer, policy advisor.

- **Health Sciences (Biomedical Research, Genetics, Public Health)**
  - *Why?* Women dominate healthcare roles but are underrepresented in **research and leadership**. Pandemics, aging populations, and biotech innovations make this field critical.
  - *Careers:* Epidemiologist, clinical researcher, health tech entrepreneur.

---
### **2. Business & Finance – Leadership & Entrepreneurship**
Women are **underrepresented in C-suite roles** (only 8% of Fortune 500 CEOs are women). If you’re strategic, analytical, or passionate about economics:
- **Finance / Economics**
  - *Why?* High earning potential, but women are only ~20% of finance professionals. Fields like **impact investing** or **fintech** need diverse voices.
  - *Careers:* Investment banker, financial analyst, venture capitalist.

- **Entrepreneurship / Business Administration**
  - *Why?* Women-owned businesses are growing fast, but face funding gaps. Learn to pitch, scale, and lead.
  - *Careers:* Startup founder, consultant, corporate leader (aim for MBA later).

- **Supply Chain Management / Operations**
  - *Why?* Critical for global trade and sustainability. Women are rare here—companies want to change that.

---
### **3. Social Sciences & Human-Centered Fields – Advocacy & Change**
If you’re passionate about **people, justice, or behavior**, these fields let you drive societal progress:
- **Psychology / Neuroscience**
  - *Why?* Mental health awareness is rising, and research on **women’s brain health** (e.g., Alzheimer’s, depression) is underfunded.
  - *Careers:* Clinical psychologist, UX researcher, HR specialist.

- **International Relations / Political Science**
  - *Why?* Women are underrepresented in **diplomacy and policy** (e.g., only ~10% of world leaders are women). Focus on **human rights, climate policy, or conflict resolution**.
  - *Careers:* Diplomat, NGO director, policy analyst.

- **Sociology / Gender Studies**
  - *Why?* Critical for addressing inequality. Pair with law, tech, or business for applied impact (e.g., **DEI—Diversity, Equity, Inclusion** roles).
  - *Careers:* Social researcher, nonprofit leader, corporate DEI officer.

---
### **4. Creative & Communication Fields – Storytelling & Design**
If you’re artistic or love **writing, design, or media**, these fields are evolving with tech:
- **Digital Marketing / Communications**
  - *Why?* Every industry needs **storytellers** (social media, branding, PR). Women excel here but often hit glass ceilings in leadership.
  - *Careers:* Content strategist, brand manager, influencer marketer.

- **Graphic Design / UX/UI Design**
  - *Why?* Tech companies need designers who understand **diverse user experiences** (e.g., designing for women, non-English speakers).
  - *Careers:* Product designer, art director, freelance creative.

- **Film / Media Studies**
  - *Why?* Only **20% of directors** are women—your stories matter! Focus on **documentaries, animation, or virtual reality**.
  - *Careers:* Filmmaker, producer, media critic.

---
### **5. Emerging & Interdisciplinary Fields – Future-Proof Careers**
Combine multiple interests for unique paths:
- **Climate Science / Sustainability**
  - *Why?* Women are **more likely to be affected by climate change** but underrepresented in leadership. Study **environmental policy, renewable energy, or sustainable fashion**.
  - *Careers:* Sustainability consultant, urban planner, green tech entrepreneur.

- **Bioethics / AI Ethics**
  - *Why?* Who decides how AI or genetic engineering is used? **Women’s voices are crucial** in shaping ethical tech.
  - *Careers:* Ethicist for tech companies, policy advisor.

- **Health Tech / Digital Health**
  - *Why?* Femtech (female-focused health tech) is a **$50B industry** but lacks female founders. Think **menstrual health apps, fertility tech, or mental health platforms**.
  - *Careers:* Product manager at a health startup, telemedicine specialist.

---
### **6. Trades & Skilled Professions – High Pay, Low Debt**
Not all paths require a 4-year degree! **Trade schools or certifications** can lead to lucrative careers with less student debt:
- **Electrician / Plumber / HVAC Technician**
  - *Why?* **<3% of tradespeople are women**, but demand is skyrocketing. Earn $50K–$100K/year with apprenticeships.
- **Aviation (Pilot, Air Traffic Controller)**
  - *Why?* Only **5% of pilots are women**. Airlines offer scholarships for female aviators.
- **Coding Bootcamps (3–6 months)**
  - *Why?* Faster than a CS degree. Many tech companies hire bootcamp grads (e.g., **Flatiron School, General Assembly**).

---
### **How to Choose? Ask Yourself:**
1. **What problems do I want to solve?** (e.g., climate change, healthcare access, inequality)
2. **What skills do I enjoy?** (e.g., writing, coding, debating, building)
3. **What lifestyle do I want?** (e.g., remote work, travel, stability, creativity)
4. **Where do I want to be in 10 years?** (e.g., CEO, researcher, artist, entrepreneur)

---
### **Final Tips for Women in University:**
- **Seek mentors:** Join groups like **Society of Women Engineers (SWE)**, **Ellevate Network**, or **Lean In**.
- **Negotiate:** Women are less likely to negotiate salaries—**start practicing now** (even for internships).
- **Double major/minor:** Pair a "passion" field (e.g., art) with a "practical" one (e.g., business) for versatility.
- **Study abroad:** Build global networks and cultural fluency.
- **Learn tech basics:** Even in non-STEM fields, **Excel, SQL, or Python** can boost your resume.

- **First year:** Take intro courses in **psychology, computer science, economics, and writing** to explore.
- **Summer:** Try internships or shadow professionals in fields you’re curious about.
- **Gap year?** Work, travel, or volunteer to gain clarity.
