Choosing what to study at university depends on your **interests, strengths, career goals, and personal values**. Since you didn’t specify your passions or skills, I’ll break this down into **high-demand fields, future-proof careers, and fulfilling paths**—with options for different personality types.

---

### **1. High-Income & Future-Proof Fields (Job Security + Salary)**
If financial stability and career growth are priorities, consider these fields:

#### **A. STEM (Science, Tech, Engineering, Math)**
- **Computer Science / Software Engineering**
  - *Why?* Tech is booming (AI, cybersecurity, cloud computing). High salaries ($80K–$200K+), remote work options.
  - *Careers:* Software developer, AI engineer, data scientist, cybersecurity expert.
  - *Skills Needed:* Coding (Python, Java, C++), problem-solving, math logic.

- **Electrical/Computer Engineering**
  - *Why?* Hardware + software (robotics, semiconductors, IoT). Critical for tech infrastructure.
  - *Careers:* Chip designer, robotics engineer, embedded systems.

- **Data Science / Artificial Intelligence**
  - *Why?* Companies need data-driven decisions. AI is transforming industries.
  - *Careers:* Data analyst, machine learning engineer, AI researcher.
  - *Skills:* Stats, Python/R, SQL, machine learning.

- **Biotechnology / Bioengineering**
  - *Why?* Growth in healthcare (gene editing, vaccines, medical devices).
  - *Careers:* Biotech researcher, pharmaceutical scientist, genetic counselor.

- **Aerospace Engineering**
  - *Why?* Space exploration (SpaceX, NASA) and aviation are expanding.
  - *Careers:* Aerospace engineer, drone designer, astronaut (long shot but cool).

#### **B. Business & Finance**
- **Finance / Economics**
  - *Why?* Always in demand (banking, investing, corporate finance).
  - *Careers:* Investment banker, financial analyst, hedge fund manager.
  - *Tip:* Pair with a **minor in CS** (fintech is huge).

- **Entrepreneurship**
  - *Why?* If you want to start a business (tech, e-commerce, consulting).
  - *Skills:* Sales, marketing, networking, risk-taking.

- **Supply Chain / Operations Management**
  - *Why?* Global logistics are complex (Amazon, Tesla need this).
  - *Careers:* Logistics manager, operations analyst.

#### **C. Healthcare (Stable & Rewarding)**
- **Medicine (MD/DO)**
  - *Why?* High prestige, job security, but **long/expensive** (8+ years).
  - *Specialties:* Surgery, psychiatry, radiology (high pay).

- **Nursing (BSN)**
  - *Why?* High demand, good pay ($70K–$120K), flexible schedules.

- **Public Health / Epidemiology**
  - *Why?* Pandemics made this field critical. Work in govt, NGOs, or research.

- **Pharmacy (PharmD)**
  - *Why?* Stable, high earning potential ($120K+), but **saturated in some areas**.

#### **D. Law (If You Love Debate & Critical Thinking)**
- **Law School (JD)**
  - *Why?* High earning potential (corporate law, IP law), but **competitive**.
  - *Tip:* Specialize in **tech law, intellectual property, or environmental law** for better prospects.

---

### **2. Creative & Unconventional Paths (Passion-Driven)**
If money isn’t the only factor and you want fulfillment:

#### **A. Arts & Design**
- **Graphic Design / UX/UI Design**
  - *Why?* Digital products need great designers. Freelance potential.
  - *Skills:* Adobe Suite, Figma, user research.

- **Film / Animation / Game Design**
  - *Why?* If you love storytelling (Pixar, Netflix, indie games).
  - *Careers:* Director, animator, game developer.

- **Architecture**
  - *Why?* Blend of art + engineering. Sustainable design is growing.
  - *Downside:* Long hours, competitive.

#### **B. Social Sciences & Humanities**
- **Psychology**
  - *Why?* Understanding human behavior (therapy, HR, marketing).
  - *Careers:* Clinical psychologist (PhD required), UX researcher, HR specialist.

- **Philosophy / Ethics**
  - *Why?* Teaches critical thinking (useful in law, tech ethics, policy).
  - *Surprise:* Many tech CEOs (e.g., Peter Thiel) studied philosophy.

- **International Relations / Political Science**
  - *Why?* If you love geopolitics, diplomacy, or NGOs.
  - *Careers:* Diplomat, policy analyst, journalist.

#### **C. Trades & Skilled Labor (No Degree Needed, High Demand)**
If you prefer **hands-on work** and **avoiding student debt**:
- **Electrician** ($60K–$100K)
- **Plumber** ($50K–$90K)
- **HVAC Technician** ($50K–$80K)
- **Welding / Construction Management** ($70K–$120K)
- *Bonus:* Many trades pay for apprenticeships (earn while you learn).

- **Quantum Computing** (Next-gen tech, still niche but exploding)
- **Cybersecurity** (Hacking defense, ethical hacking)
- **Neuroscience** (Brain-computer interfaces, AI + biology)
- **Urban Planning / Smart Cities** (Sustainable infrastructure)
- **Blockchain / Web3** (Crypto, decentralized finance—risky but high upside)

---

### **4. How to Decide? Ask Yourself:**
✅ **What subjects do I enjoy?** (Math, writing, building, helping people?)
✅ **Do I prefer hands-on work or desk jobs?**
✅ **Am I okay with long hours/study?** (Medicine, law, engineering = intense)
✅ **Do I want job security or flexibility?** (Tech vs. freelance art)
✅ **Can I handle student debt?** (Avoid expensive degrees unless ROI is clear)

---
### **5. Pro Tips for Choosing:**
- **Double Major or Minor:** Pair a **practical major (CS, Business)** with a **passion (Music, Psychology)**.
- **Internships Matter More Than Major:** Many jobs care about **skills > degree name**.
- **Gap Year?** If unsure, take a year to work, travel, or try coding bootcamps.
- **Avoid "Dead-End" Degrees:** Unless you’re passionate, be cautious with:
  - Fine Arts (without a clear career path)
  - Generic "Studies" degrees (e.g., "Communications" without a specialty)
  - Hospitality (unless you want to manage hotels/restaurants)
