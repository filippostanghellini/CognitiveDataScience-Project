Choosing what to study at university is a big decision and depends on your interests, strengths, career goals, and personal values. Here are some recommendations and factors to consider, broken down by broad categories of study:

### 1. **STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics)**
   - **Computer Science/Software Engineering**: High demand, excellent job prospects, and opportunities in fields like AI, cybersecurity, and software development.
   - **Engineering (Mechanical, Electrical, Civil, etc.)**: Offers a wide range of career options, strong earning potential, and the ability to work on innovative projects.
   - **Data Science/Statistics**: Growing field with applications in almost every industry, from finance to healthcare.
   - **Physics or Applied Mathematics**: Great for analytical thinkers, with opportunities in research, finance, and tech.
   - **Biotechnology/Bioengineering**: Combines biology and technology, with applications in medicine, agriculture, and environmental science.

   **Best if**: You enjoy problem-solving, are good at math, and like working with technology or scientific concepts.

### 2. **Business and Economics**
   - **Business Administration/Management**: Versatile degree with career paths in finance, marketing, consulting, and entrepreneurship.
   - **Finance/Accounting**: Strong job prospects in banking, investment, corporate finance, or accounting firms.
   - **Economics**: Develops analytical and critical thinking skills, useful in policy-making, finance, and research.
   - **Entrepreneurship**: Ideal if you want to start your own business or work in startups.

   **Best if**: You enjoy leadership, strategy, working with numbers, or have an interest in how businesses operate.

### 3. **Health and Medicine**
   - **Medicine (Pre-Med)**: Long but rewarding path to becoming a doctor, surgeon, or specialist.
   - **Nursing**: High demand, meaningful work, and opportunities for specialization.
   - **Pharmacy or Pharmacology**: Focus on drugs, their effects, and patient care.
   - **Public Health**: Work on improving community health, policy-making, or global health initiatives.
   - **Sports Science/Physical Therapy**: Ideal if you're interested in fitness, rehabilitation, or sports performance.

   **Best if**: You have a passion for helping others, enjoy science (especially biology/chemistry), and are comfortable with rigorous study.

### 4. **Social Sciences and Humanities**
   - **Psychology**: Understand human behavior, with career paths in counseling, HR, or research.
   - **Political Science/International Relations**: Great for careers in government, diplomacy, or NGOs.
   - **Sociology/Anthropology**: Study societies and cultures, useful in social work, research, or policy.
   - **Philosophy**: Develops critical thinking and logic; useful in law, academia, or writing.
   - **History**: Strong foundation for careers in education, law, journalism, or museum work.

   **Best if**: You enjoy understanding people, societies, or historical contexts, and have strong communication skills.

### 5. **Creative Fields**
   - **Graphic Design/Digital Media**: Growing demand for digital content creators, UX/UI designers, and animators.
   - **Film/Media Studies**: Pursue careers in filmmaking, journalism, or content production.
   - **Fine Arts/Architecture**: For those passionate about visual arts, design, or building structures.
   - **Music or Performing Arts**: Ideal if you have a strong passion for performance, composition, or production.

   **Best if**: You are creative, enjoy expressing yourself artistically, and are comfortable with a potentially competitive job market.

### 6. **Law and Criminal Justice**
   - **Law (Pre-Law)**: Path to becoming a lawyer, judge, or legal consultant.
   - **Criminal Justice**: Careers in law enforcement, corrections, or forensic science.
   - **Forensic Psychology**: Combine psychology and law to work in criminal profiling or rehabilitation.

   **Best if**: You enjoy debate, critical analysis, and have an interest in justice or legal systems.

### 7. **Environmental and Agricultural Sciences**
   - **Environmental Science/Sustainability**: Work on climate change, conservation, or renewable energy.
   - **Agriculture/Agronomy**: Focus on food production, farming technology, or agricultural economics.

   **Best if**: You care about the environment, sustainability, or food security.

### 8. **Trades and Vocational Skills (Alternative to University)**
   - If university isn’t appealing, consider vocational training in fields like:
     - **Electrician/Plumber/HVAC Technician**: High demand, good pay, and job security.
     - **Welding/Construction Management**: Hands-on work with strong earning potential.
     - **Aviation (Pilot or Air Traffic Control)**: Specialized training with exciting career paths.

### Key Questions to Help You Decide:
1. **What are your passions?** What subjects or activities do you enjoy most?
2. **What are your strengths?** Are you analytical, creative, hands-on, or a strong communicator?
3. **What lifestyle do you want?** Do you prefer a 9-5 job, flexible hours, travel, or remote work?
4. **Job market and earnings**: Some fields (like STEM or healthcare) have higher demand and salaries, while others (like arts) may require more networking or entrepreneurship.
5. **Long-term goals**: Do you want to climb the corporate ladder, start your own business, or make a social impact?

### Practical Tips:
- **Explore before committing**: Take online courses (e.g., Coursera, edX) or internships in fields you're curious about.
- **Double major or minor**: Combine interests (e.g., Computer Science + Psychology for AI ethics, or Business + Environmental Science for sustainability consulting).
- **Talk to professionals**: Reach out to people in careers you’re considering for advice (LinkedIn is great for this).
- **Consider hybrid fields**: Emerging areas like **AI Ethics, Cybersecurity, Renewable Energy, or Health Tech** combine multiple disciplines.

### Trending Fields (2024 and Beyond):
- **Artificial Intelligence/Machine Learning**
- **Renewable Energy/Green Technology**
- **Healthcare Technology (Telemedicine, Biotech)**
- **Cybersecurity**
- **Data Analytics/Big Data**
- **Mental Health Counseling**

### Final Thought:
There’s no "one-size-fits-all" answer. The best choice aligns with **your** interests, skills, and goals. If you're unsure, start with a broader degree (e.g., Business, Psychology, or General Engineering) and specialize later.
